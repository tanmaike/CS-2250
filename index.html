Soft Chocolate Sugar Cookies

These Sugar Cookies are a party of favority of many, with a rich flavor that's complemented by a light sweetness. I've made these treats dozens of times, and every time, they're gone within the hour.

These cookies are highly reliant on its main ingredient: cocoa powder. It's best to not concede to using a cheaper cocoa. The richer the cocoa, the deeper the flavor. I personally recommend using King Arthur Baking Company's Black Cocoa. Its wonderful flavor is made even more wonderful by its fair price.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 Cups (~336g) of All-Purpose Flour
  • 3/4 Cups of (~72g) Unsweetened Cocoa Powder
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of Baking Soda
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of Baking Powder
  • 3/4 Teaspoons of Salt
  • 1/2 Cup of Unsalted Butter, melted
  • 1/2 Cup of Unsalted Butter, room temperature
  • 1 Cup (~144g) of Brown Sugar
  • 1/2 Cup (~115g) of Granulated Sugar, plus an additional 1/3 Cup (~77g)
  • 2 Large Eggs
  • 1/2 Tablespoon of Vanilla Extract
  • 1/2 Teaspoon of Espresso Powder

Process

  1. In a bowl, whisk together the cocoa powder, flour, baking soda, baking powder, and salt. Set aside for later.
  2. In a second bowl, beat the butter with both sugars until it is light and fluffy.
  3. Add in the eggs, one at a time, into the butter and mix well.
  4. Add in the vanilla extract and espresso powder until well combined.
  5. Slowly add the flour mixture into the second bowl and mix until just combined.
  6. Cover the dough with plastic wrap and refrigerate it for at least an hour.
  7. Preheat the oven to 350Â°F.
  8. Using either your hands or an ice cream scooper, scoop balls of dough and roll into the additional granulated sugar before onto a parchment lined baking sheet.
  9. Place cookies into the oven and bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the edges are set.
  10. Take cookies out of the oven and let them cool on the baking sheet for at least five minutes before transferring to a wire rack to finish cooling.
